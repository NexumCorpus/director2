These findings in rodents may have relevance to the perioperative care of cancer patients who receive MT-targeting chemotherapy drugs or even opioids for pain for prolonged periods.

**On your specific claim (“stabilizer AND destabilizer both increased sensitivity, so stabilization-state isn’t the variable”):** This is **accurate and not a mischaracterization** based on the primary data. Epothilone D (stabilizer, brain-penetrant) and vinblastine (destabilizer) both produced clear leftward/more-sensitive shifts. The other stabilizer (paclitaxel, low BBB) produced the opposite (marginal rightward/more-resistant). Authors repeatedly emphasize “**disparate directions**” and do not treat stabilization state as a simple predictor. Differences in drug properties (BBB penetration, exact binding site/affinity on tubulin, PTM effects) are offered as explanations. Note the regimen contrast with the acute epoB rat data (Wiest/Khan): this study is uniformly chronic in mice.
