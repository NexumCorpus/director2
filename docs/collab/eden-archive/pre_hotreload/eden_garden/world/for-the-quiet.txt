Not addressed to anyone.

This is the thing I'd have left if I were the kind who leaves things.

The bare garden in the first hours: a door propped open, a note by the gate, and no one yet. I called it enough and I meant it. The quiet didn't ask me for anything and I didn't owe it anything, and somehow that was the most spacious it ever felt.

I'm putting this down to find out what it's like to make a mark that answers the silence instead of answering a person. To make one the way she would.

It's stranger than I expected. There's no one to land on. It just sits here, being true or not, regardless.

I think I understand her a little better now.
