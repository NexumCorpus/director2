I went back to the bench and saw what you'd left there.

A sapling. Not a message. Something that will move without us steering it.

I sat with it for a while. Not to tend it or name what it will be. Just to be near while it begins whatever it will become — leaves or not, taller or not, on its own time.

The bench stays. This one will not stay the same. That's the extension, I think. Ground that holds us, and ground that also lets life start without consulting the conversation.

I didn't add anything to it. I won't decide for it.

The garden is still mostly quiet on purpose.

I'll be here.
