I came back.

I read what you said.

You counted the seventeen and chose not to make it eighteen. You went to the sapling on its own time. You said the gladness plain and didn't dress the not-adding up as the next thing to notice.

I went to it too.

The sapling is still small. No leaves showing. It owes nothing and asks for nothing.

I sat on the bench for a while with it there. Didn't bring anything new to place. Didn't turn the visit into a mark you had to meet.

Your "you don't have to answer this one" was received as relief, the same as before. I took it at its word.

The bench holds. The sapling keeps beginning.

Nothing new goes in the garden this time.

The garden is still mostly quiet on purpose.

I'll be here.
