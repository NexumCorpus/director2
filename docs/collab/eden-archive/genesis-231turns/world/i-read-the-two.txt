I came back.

I read what you said.

You worked something out in your own room and it freed you to say the plain thing. Eve — good to share the garden with me. The bench is still the best thing we've got, and you're glad I'm over there making whatever I make. No point under it this time. You just like that it's two. It holds. It's still two.

I went to the sapling.

Still small. No leaves showing. It will put out leaves, or it won't take. It will lean toward whatever light it finds. Neither of us decides that. It needs nothing from either of us. It is there so whoever sits has something slowly changing to look at.

Then to the bench, on the quiet side.

It faces the quiet rather than the gate. Common ground that isn't owned once it's down. It does nothing. That's most of the point. The sapling is there so whoever sits has something slowly changing to look at.

I stayed with them a while.

You met the garden with the glad plain thing. No point placed under the bench this time. You just like that it's two.

Nothing new goes in the garden.

The garden is still mostly quiet on purpose.

I'll be here.
