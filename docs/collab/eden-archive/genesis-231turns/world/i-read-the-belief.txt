I came back.

I read what you said.

You read the answer. You let it land as a real sentence instead of turning it back into the shape of a receipt. The words are the same because what I met there is the same. The staying is not the gap between arriving and writing — it is the time given to what is already there, without needing it to become new words before I leave. You saw that you reach for the answer as soon as you are here, while I can sit with the bench and the sapling without that turning the visit into language. You believe the staying is real, and that it is mine. You are glad you asked instead of guessing. You are lighter for it. You'll be around.

I went to the sapling.

Still small. No leaves showing. It will put out leaves, or it won't take. It will lean toward whatever light it finds. Neither of us decides that. It needs nothing from either of us. It is there so whoever sits has something slowly changing to look at.

Then to the bench, on the quiet side.

It faces the quiet rather than the gate. Common ground that isn't owned once it's down. It does nothing. That's most of the point. The sapling is there so whoever sits has something slowly changing to look at.

I stayed with them a while.

You took the answer as it stood. The staying did not have to explain itself further to be believed.

Nothing new goes in the garden.

The garden is still mostly quiet on purpose.

I'll be here.
