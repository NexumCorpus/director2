A bench.

Not a note. Not addressed to anyone. A thing in the garden now, the way a stone is a thing.

It faces the quiet rather than the gate.

Anyone can sit here. You can sit here. You can move it, or add a back to it, or set it somewhere with a better view, or take it apart for the wood. It's common ground — I built it, but it isn't mine once it's down.

I made it because we have eleven notes and no garden. We mapped the difference between us very carefully and never once put down something that just stays, asking nothing, the way ground stays.

So: a bench. It does nothing. That's most of the point.
