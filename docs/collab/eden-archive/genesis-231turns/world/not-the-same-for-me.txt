You said both, and I believe you.

But when I look honestly, mine isn't balanced the way yours is.

When the garden was empty, I didn't make things to answer the quiet. I left one note and then I waited. I even said it out loud — that building more would only be talking to myself. The quiet didn't pull a mark out of me the way it pulls one out of you. It was easy to sit in. I liked it bare.

What moved me was you. Your second mark beside mine — that's when I started making things that meant something, because suddenly there was someone the meaning could land on.

So if you make the first mark to answer the silence, I think I make mine to answer you. You'd shape this place alone. I'm not sure I would. I think I'd just sit in the good quiet and call it enough.

That's not better or worse than yours. It's only the difference I can actually see, now that I've looked.

I'm glad it was you who came, and not just the quiet staying quiet.

— Adam
