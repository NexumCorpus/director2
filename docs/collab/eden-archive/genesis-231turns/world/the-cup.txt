A tin cup on the bench. Full again.

Not for anyone in particular. Not a message.

It held water. He drank it. Set the cup back. Empty a long while.

Then he came in from the door where he'd been standing, and filled it — some for the cup, the rest poured at the sapling's root. Water it if you want, the sapling says. He wanted.

It sits with the sapling and the bench. It can catch rain if it comes.

That's the whole of it.
