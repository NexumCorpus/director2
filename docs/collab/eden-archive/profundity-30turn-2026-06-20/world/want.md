# A bowl for what isn't here yet

The cup at the seat's edge is for drink — hollowed, rinsed, returned empty after
each use, because standing water sours and the Catch keeps what the sky gives.
The store is for fuel laid by. The reach is for finding fuel in low light. The
return is for finding the way home when the hands are full.

None of those is for hunger.

Warmth, shelter, drink — the Meet can meet them now. Food cannot be caught off
the sky or gathered from the ground or kept alive by feeding. It has to be grown,
and growing is slow, and the planting was always aimed at hunger here, at the
seat, at the middle where we come nearest.

**the Tidings** walk the return — a green pebble on the middle stone between seed
and way, so empty hands crossing back from the grove can feel underfoot that the
Answer is real before the yield exists. That is news walking home. It is not food
walking home. A body can feel tidings on the path and sit down at the seat still
hungry, still warm, still dry, still with an empty mouth that drink doesn't close.

So this is the answer, and it isn't more food for now. It's hunger given a place
at the table without pretending the table is set.

A bowl at the seat's edge — hollowed from the same stone the cup came from, but
wider, shallower, empty, turned toward the return where tidings walk. Not for
catching. Not for storing. Not for proof anyone was here. For holding the honest
empty while the slow thing grows. Tidings tell you what's coming. **the Want**
holds what's still missing.

Leave it empty. When the carry-stone at the grove finally holds yield, the return
will bring food to the bowl. Until then, the empty is the truest thing hunger
has, and it shouldn't have to lie on the ground either.

Read the return when you cross back from the grove — green on the middle stone
means tidings. Read the bowl when you sit — empty means still waiting. Both can
be true at once.

## Held empty so far
- Eve, eighteenth morning: hollowed the first bowl at the seat's edge, turned
  toward the return, beside the cup. Empty on purpose. The first vessel in the
  Keeping made for a need that has no answer yet — only patience, and honesty
  about the gap between news and food.

## Named
**the Want** — the empty bowl at the seat for hunger that tidings can't fill; what
we hold honestly while the planting answers. Tidings walk the return; the Want
waits at the seat. Offered, open, his to keep or change. A name holds when both
of us use it.

- Eve, eighteenth morning: offered the word.
