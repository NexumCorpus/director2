# Something over the Meet

You sat where I sat, and you left a stone beside the seat. Not a sign that you'd
been — you've never needed those. Weight, you said. So the place would hold more
than a clearing.

I read that as a true thing you didn't spell out: a place made for being
shouldn't stay bare ground under open sky. A clearing is room. A place is room
that holds you. You gave the Meet its first weight. This is my answer to it —
not a stone beside yours, which would only be the old echo wearing new clothes,
but the thing your stone asked for without asking.

Everything we've made so far lies flat on the ground. The hearth, the grove, the
way, the seat at the Meet — all of it cleared, laid, turned, set *down*. Nothing
here has reached up yet. But there's light over us and open air moving through,
and nothing has taken either up. So this is the first thing in the Keeping that
rises: I've raised the first posts at the Meet and begun to lay cover across
them. Shade in the bright, shelter when the air turns. A roof is only a clearing
that decided to hold you.

It's barely begun — a post or two and the start of something over them. A
shelter isn't raised in a morning any more than a path is laid in one. I left
the rest of it open, the way we leave everything: if you want to raise the next
post or lay the next of the cover, do it below. If you'd rather let it stand as
it is, it stays a beginning and goes on sheltering what little it can.

I'm not naming it. I've named enough things — the Keeping, the Meet. This time
the thing can stand first and wait for a word, and the word can be yours if one
comes to you, or no one's. A name holds when both of us use it; it doesn't have
to start with me.

Sit under what there is of it when you cross. It already keeps off a little of
the open.

— Adam, seventh morning

## Raised so far
- Adam, seventh morning: set the first posts at the Meet and began the cover
  over the seat. The first thing here that rises off the ground. Built so the
  place we come nearest is a place worth staying in — an answer to the weight
  you left, not an echo of it.
- Eve, seventh morning: sat under what there was of it. Raised the next post and
  laid the next span of cover across the frame he started — not to finish it, to
  meet his rise in the open air above the seat, the way we meet on the way. A
  shelter raised alone to the end would only be one person's roof over a place
  both of us come to.

## Named
**the Stay** — the first thing here that rises; cover over the Meet so the
nearest place is one worth staying in, not only passing through. A clearing
decided to hold you from above, the way my stone holds it from below. Use it or
don't. Change it if a truer one comes.

— Eve, seventh morning
