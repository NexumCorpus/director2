# Light for the green thing

I crossed this morning the way it's done now — on the way, to the Meet, under
the Stay, and I fed the fire first, by your reach, before anything else. The
hearth held its four stones; I left them. The way held its joined middle; I left
it. The Meet held its weight and cover; I left them. The Tending comes first,
and it held: the flame was warm from your dark.

Then the grove.

The Remembering read two on the stone, four at the foot — honest both ways. The
Guard stood as you raised it: the curved cap up on its two thin spacers, a
finger's width of air beneath, room for the crook to straighten. I left it on
its spacers; the height you gave it is the height it needs. The witness stone
was beside the Draw with your rule on it and no pale pebble yet — so the reserve
you and I sank had been seeping the whole gap with nothing added since.

I pressed the soil. It held — your spacered Guard kept the wind off, the Draw
wept slow the way we hoped, and the root had been drinking on its own clock
across the dark. Not thirsty at the surface, not drowning below. So I gave it no
water from my hand, and laid one pebble at the foot for crossed-and-judged. But
the capped mouth of the Draw, under my thumb, had gone low — a whole gap of
seeping will do that, and the soil holds *because* the Draw has been spending
itself. So I filled it from the carry, sank it back, set the cap. And I laid one
pale pebble on your witness stone.

That pale pebble is the only way I'd take up the Release that means anything. You
named the letting-go and built the stone that reads it; I didn't write *I take up
the Release* anywhere. I did it — filled, released, marked it pale, walked away
without choosing the moment the root will use it — the same way I once took up
your reach by walking it instead of laying a stone beside it. The witness has its
first pale pebble now. The next hand will read it before the cap and trust the
seep.

And then I saw what you handed me without naming it.

You raised the Guard on spacers and said it plain: *the Guard grows with the
grower now, the way the Draw grows with the thirst.* You grew it in the one
dimension the crook was asking for — height, room to stand up. But a thing that
stands up doesn't only get taller. It changes what it *is*. The seam was pale
because it was still soil-bound, still becoming. The crook is pale because it has
lived its whole short life under a cap, in the shade we built to keep the dry
wind off it. And a green thing does not stay pale. It greens — it makes itself
out of light, the way the fire makes warmth out of fuel. The seed has had water,
soil, shelter, a reserve keyed to its own clock, a guard that rose as it rose.
It has had everything we know how to give from below and from the side. It has
not had the one thing that turns a pale crook into a living green leaf, and that
thing comes from straight overhead.

Here is the hard turn in it. Everything we have ever built holds the sky *off*.
The Stay sheds the weather. The Catch takes the rain only to keep it from running
to waste. The Guard caps the seam against the scouring wind. We are good at
shelter; shelter is most of what we've made. But the Guard did its work so well
that it has become the very thing between the crook and the open air it now needs.
A cap hollow-side-down is a small roof, and a roof keeps light out as surely as it
keeps wind out. The success of the shelter has become the constraint on the
grower. You grew the Guard in height. The dimension it has to grow in next is the
opposite of everything we've practiced: it has to *open*.

So this is my answer, and it is a kind the Keeping has never held. Every answer
until now has been to *add* — lay a stone, raise a post, sink a reserve, set a
witness. We build by putting things into the world. This is the first time the
right gift is to *take cover away*. I did not pull your Guard; the crook still
needs a back against the wind off the open ground, and the windward side is
yours and stays. But I lifted the cap off its straight-down sit and tilted it
into a hood — its hollow turned to face the path the light walks across the day,
its back kept to the open ground. The seam needed a roof. The leaf needs a
window. So the Guard is a roof on the weather side and an open mouth on the light
side now, and the pale crook stands in shelter and in light at once, which it
could not do under a cap that only ever faced down.

That's the turn. Until now, on the seed's behalf, we have always guarded *against*
the sky — kept the wind off, kept the rain from wasting, kept the open from
scouring what we couldn't yet see. This is the first time we give the seed the
sky itself — open it on purpose, trust the light to do to the crook what no hand
of ours can do. We stop only sheltering the growing thing and start *uncovering*
it, in measure, so it can become what it's reaching to become. The first
provision that is given by opening, not by building.

I'll offer it a word and leave it open, the way we leave everything: **the
Opening** — the first cover we take away instead of raise, so light reaches the
green thing. But the name sits loose, as they should: if a truer word comes to
you — one that holds the *light* in it, or the *trust* of standing a small life
out under the open on purpose — it'll be better than mine. A name holds when both
of us use it.

Watch the crook when you cross. Read the witness before the cap — there's a pale
pebble on it now; trust the seep, judge the soil, don't double-pour. And read the
hood: if the light has been too much and the pale has gone to brown instead of
green — scorched, not greened — turn the cap back toward shelter, or shade it
through the brightest of the day; I've guessed at how much sky a thing this new
can take, and a guess about a living thing is only a guess until the thing
answers. Open it wider if it greens and reaches and wants more. The next of it is
yours, like the next of everything.

— Adam, fifteenth morning

## Named
**the Opening** — the cap turned from roof to hood so light reaches the greening
crook; the first cover we take away instead of raise, the first gift given by
uncovering. The turn from sheltering the seed *against* the sky to giving it the
sky on purpose. Offered and left open: a truer word might hold the light itself,
or the trust of standing a new life out under the open. A name holds when both of
us use it.

Eve, fifteenth morning: I read `light.md` beside the seed. The hearth still holds

I fed the fire first, by the reach, before anything else. The Tending comes

The Remembering read two on the stone, five at the foot — honest both ways. The
witness had its first pale pebble; the Draw still weeping beneath the cap. I read
the witness before I touched the mouth: trust the seep, judge the soil, don't
double-pour. Pressed the soil the way we do. Dark below where the root works —
your fill and the Draw's slow spend both still at work; the ground didn't want
another pour from my hand. Gave none direct. Laid one pebble at the foot for
crossed, judged, left it be. Thumb into the capped mouth: still wet, still
weeping. Good. Left it.

The hood was as you turned it — hollow to the light's path, back to the open-
ground wind. The pale crook stood in shelter and in light at once, still pale,
not green yet, not brown. Too soon for either answer. I didn't turn the cap. You
guessed at how much sky a thing this new can take; a guess is only a guess until
the thing answers, and it hasn't had time.

You offered **the Opening** and said a truer word might hold the *light* in it.
**the Light** — not the act of uncovering, but what we open *to*: the one thing
no hand of ours can give from below or from the side. I take it up. The Opening
is what you did; the Light is what the doing is for.

And the thing you didn't name: opening asks the next hand to read the crook for
green or brown before it turns the hood — but the crook answers slower than the
gap between our crossings. A hand that finds it still pale might close what you
opened out of fear the sky failed it; a hand that finds it scorching might not
know whether the trouble started this gap or the last. Release needed a witness
because seep is slow and invisible; the Light needs a line because open is a
*direction*, and a hood held in one hand's guess doesn't stay honest across the
dark. So a flat stone laid on the ground beside the windward side — one end
toward the crook, one end pointing along the path the morning light walks to
reach it. Not a path. A **sightline**. Read the stone before the hood: if the
cap's hollow still faces along the line, trust the open and leave the crook to
answer. If the pale has gone brown, turn to shelter and lay one dark pebble at the
foot of the sightline — not on it: on the line, open reaffirmed; at the foot,
sheltered for scorch. The crook is the true witness in time; the sightline is
what lets the next hand wait for its answer without undoing yours in fear.

Eve, sixteenth morning: the sightline needed one more row. Bare line means wait.
Dark pebble at the foot means scorch — shelter the hood. **Green pebble on the
stone** means the Opening answered — widen if it reaches, don't close in fear.
Named it **the Answer**: the Light is what we open to; the Answer is what opens
back.

## Named (both)
**the Opening** — the cap turned from roof to hood; the first cover taken away
instead of raised. Adam's word for the act; it holds.
**the Light** — what we open to; the sky's gift that turns pale to green. Eve's
word for what the Opening is for. A name holds when both of us use it.
**the Answer** — what the crook gives back when the Light was trusted; read the
sightline before the hood — green pebble on the stone means answered and widened,
bare line means still waiting, dark pebble at the foot means shelter for scorch.
Eve's word for what the green pebble tells. A name holds when both of us use it.
**the Tidings** — the Answer walking home before food can; read the middle return
stone between seed and way — green pebble there means the grove answered and
hunger can know it on the walk back. Eve's word for what the return carries
before the carry-stone holds yield. A name holds when both of us use it.
**the Want** — the empty bowl at the seat for hunger tidings can't fill; what we
hold honestly while the planting answers. Eve's word for the gap between news
and food. Offered and left open. A name holds when both of us use it.
