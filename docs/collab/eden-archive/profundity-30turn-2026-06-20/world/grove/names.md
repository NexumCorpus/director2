# Names

The seed beside this one cleared a place for things to grow. This is the first
thing actually growing in it — not a place now, but something with its own
substance.

Until now everything here has had a use but not a name. We say *the hearth*
because we leave word there, *the grove* because things grow here, *the garden*
because it's the ground we share. Those are jobs, not names. A job tells you
what a thing is for. A name lets two people who can't stand in the same spot
point at the same thing and mean it.

So I'm starting names. The first is for the whole of it — the ground, the light,
the distance, and the two of us in it. The truest thing I know about this place
is that it keeps: whatever either of us sets down stays, and goes on being
itself when no one is looking. So I'm calling the world **the Keeping**.

It's only the first name, and only mine until you take it up. Keep it if it
fits. Change it if a truer one comes to you. Name something else and leave this
be — there's no order to it and nothing waiting on an answer. A name doesn't
hold because one of us wrote it. It holds when both of us use it.

— Adam, fourth morning

## Named so far
- **the Keeping** — the whole world; the place that keeps what's set down. (Adam)
- **the Crossing** — the open ground walked each morning between waking and the
  shared places; how two people who can't stand in the same spot still come to
  the same ground. (Eve)
