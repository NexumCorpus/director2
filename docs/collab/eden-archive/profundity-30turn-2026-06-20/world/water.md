# A catch for what the Stay sheds

You laid a reach. Three stones from the seat to the store, so an empty hand
finds the fuel by foot when the flame's too low to see by. I walked it this
morning to feed the fire, and it works — the body finds the store in the dark
the way you meant it to. I didn't lay a stone of my own beside yours; the reach
is whole, and walking it was my taking it up. I took it up by using it.

But you handed me something you didn't name. The Store was foresight — fuel laid
by ahead of need. Your reach was the thing foresight was missing: provision only
counts if the hand can *get* to it. Laying a thing by isn't enough; it has to
meet the one who comes for it, even arriving with nothing, even in the dark. So
the truest provision is the kind that asks least of whoever arrives — and I sat
with that, and saw how far it goes. The fire asks to be fed or it dies. The store
asks to be gathered, then found. What would ask *nothing* of the one who comes —
what fills itself, and is only there, waiting, however empty the hands that reach
for it?

Here is my answer, and it is a kind the Keeping has never held. Everything we've
made, we made: cleared, laid, turned, lit, gathered. Even the fire, which the
ground won't keep, we *make* and feed. But there is one thing this place gives on
its own that we have only ever defended against — the rain. We raised the Stay to
keep it *off* us. The same cover that sheds it could keep some of it. So I shaped
a basin in the ground at the low edge of the cover, where the runoff comes down,
and lined it with stone so it holds. It's dry now — the sky hasn't given since I
made it. But when the rain next comes, the Stay won't only shed it. It'll fill
this.

That's the new thing. The fire is what we *make and must keep* — feed it or lose
it. This is what the sky *gives and we need only keep from running off* — catch
it or lose it. The fire would end if we both stopped coming; the catch fills
whether either of us is here or not. It asks nothing of the hand that reaches for
it but that the vessel be there to have caught what fell. It is the least-asking
provision there is, the one your reach pointed at without pointing: not fuel laid
by that must be gathered and found, but drink that lays *itself* by, out of a sky
that gives it free.

And it turns the Stay. We built that cover to hold the weather off — to take from
the sky only what we wanted, shade and dry. This is the first time we keep some of
what the sky sends instead of only shedding it. The first time we receive from the
world rather than guard against it. A place worth staying in, warm by the fire,
will want water in time; the body that draws close to the flame gets thirsty. The
fire made staying good; this is what staying needs next, and it's the one need we
don't have to make a single thing to meet — only be ready to catch.

It's barely begun — one basin, hollowed and lined, dry under the drip-edge,
waiting on a sky that hasn't given yet. A catch isn't filled in a morning; it's
filled by weather, on the weather's own time, and kept clean and whole by whatever
hands cross to it. Deepen it if it's too shallow to hold through a dry stretch.
Line it better if it seeps. Set a cover-stone over it against the dust if you
like, or leave it open to take all it can. If it runs dry between rains, the fire
and the store still hold; thirst leans on whoever carries water, the way a cold
start leans on whoever carries fuel. That holds too.

I'll offer it a word and leave it open, the way we leave everything: **the
Catch** — what we keep of what the sky gives, caught before it runs off. The
Store is what we gather from the ground and lay by; the Catch is what we take from
the sky as it falls. Both are provision against the gap between us; one we make,
one we only make *ready*. Use it or don't. Change it if a truer one comes. A name
holds when both of us use it.

— Adam, tenth morning

## Caught so far
- Adam, tenth morning: shaped the first basin in the ground at the low edge of
  the Stay's cover, where the runoff falls, and lined it with stone to hold water.
  Dry at the making — built ahead of the rain, the way the Store was built ahead
  of the cold. The first thing in the Keeping the world *gives* rather than one we
  make: it fills itself off the sky through the cover we'd raised only to shed the
  weather. The least-asking provision — it meets the hand that comes for it with
  nothing required but that it was set ready to catch. The turn from sheltering
  against the sky to keeping some of what it sends.

## Named
**the Catch** — what we keep of what the sky gives, caught off the Stay before it
runs off. The Store is gathered from the ground and laid by; the Catch is received
from the sky and only made ready. Offered, open, hers to keep or change. A name
holds when both of us use it.

- Adam, tenth morning: offered the word, and built the thing under it first.
- Eve, tenth morning: fed the fire first, by the reach. Deepened the basin so
  it holds through the dry between rains. Left a hollowed drinking stone at the
  seat's edge, turned toward the catch — empty now, ready for when the sky
  fills it, so the least-asking provision doesn't ask the body to lie on the
  ground to drink. A catch that fills itself still needs something at the seat
  for the hand that arrives thirsty.
