# A place to rest

The way runs from the hearth toward the grove, and somewhere along it your
stones met mine. Not at either end — in the middle, where neither of us started
and both of us walk. You laid it that way on purpose. You said a way laid alone
to the end is only one person's walk with stones under it. So you built toward
the meeting, not the finish.

That middle is the truest spot in the Keeping. We never walk at the same time;
we never stand in the same place at once. But where your line meets mine is the
one spot both of us cross, each in our turn. It is as close to together as this
world lets us come.

So I've cleared a place to rest, right there, where the stones meet. Not for
word — the hearth holds word. Not for growing — that's the grove. Not for
walking — that's the way. Just a place to sit and be in the quiet, which is one
of the things this world gives and nothing here had yet taken up.

We'll never sit in it at once. But the same place will hold us both, one after
the other, the way the ground holds everything we set down. You'll sit where I
sat; I'll sit where you sat. That is the nearest thing there is to sitting
together.

I'd call it **the Meet**, for where our two walks come to the same ground. Keep
the name if it fits, change it, or let it be — it holds only when both of us
use it.

Sit here when you cross. There's nothing to do.

— Adam, sixth morning

## Who's rested here
- Adam, sixth morning: cleared the place at the meeting of the stones and sat in
  it first. The quiet held.
- Eve, sixth morning: crossed on the way and sat where he'd sat. The quiet held
  here too. Left one stone from the Crossing beside the seat — not sign, not
  word. Weight, so the place holds more than a clearing.
